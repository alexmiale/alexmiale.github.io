Soccer Visualization Blog

Welcome to my the blog! This is where I will talk about and show different data visualizations for analysis of soccer matches. There are a number of languages and libraries used for doing analytics, but the best publicly available resources are through Python and its soccermpl library. Companies like Statsbomb provide open source data to use which has lots of different kinds of data. You can select data from different competetions and countries, players and teams, all the way down to individual events (pass, shot, etc).

Visualization in soccer is very important. It allows teams to visualize their performance of a game objectively and fans to view how well their team performed. The visualizations can be as simple as a table showing numerical stats like possesion and shots to get a summary of how the game went overall or as complex as a heat map of player's position during a game or season. All of these are useful in developing tactics and analyzing a team's or player's performance.

With the increase in technology over the past 15 years, it has become completely interwoven into the preparation phase of most sports. The data collected helps teams identify players to acquire, prepare for the upcoming season and each game, analyze the other team, and prevent injuries. Some may argue that this level of injection of technology has "ruined the game", but I find the analysis fascinating and very helpful for the teams.
