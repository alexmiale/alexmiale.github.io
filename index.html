As a recent graduate with high honors of Northeastern University with a B.S. in Data Science and a minor in Music Technology, I am exploring my options for graduate school, research opportunities, and industry work. I have experience as a Research Assistant to Victor Zappi and have completed various projects in different data science classes like Practical Neural Networks, Information Presentation and Visualization, and Large-Scale Databases, as well as coursework in Embedded Audio Programming and Object-Oriented Design.

Prior to working towards a degree in Data Science, I began as a Computer Science major where I developed a strong understanding for software engineering and coding intensive problems. This was put to use during my research assistantship, where I developed audio plugins in C++ that run audio machine learning models in real time. My data science classes prepared me for roles in data analytics or data engineering, where I utilized Python to train and run machine learning models using libraries like PyTorch and scikit-learn, and created, queried, and maintained various SQL and NoSQL databases.

My goals for work are, at a high level, to serve communities or groups of people and improve their quality of life through data-driven decisions. I prefer communities that are close to my heart (any of New England) and any aspect that could help them out (food insecurity, disease prevention, safeguards for AI, etc.). Some titles that intrigue me for career opportunities are policy assessment for AI, network analysis, and machine learning engineer/developer. As these roles are not typical for an entry-level candidate, I am looking for data science/analytics/engineering roles and opportunities that would hone the skills necessary to succeed in the industry.
